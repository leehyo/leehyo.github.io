PurposeCam

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 14, 2026

PurposeCam is a camera app that saves photos into purpose-based spaces inside the app. We do not upload your photos, notes, or camera content to developer servers.

1. Data We Use

  • Camera: used when you choose to capture a photo.
  • Photo Library Add permission: used only when you choose to save a selected photo to Photos.
  • App storage: photos, thumbnails, space names, timestamps, notes, and app settings may be stored on your device.

2. Local Photo Storage

Photos captured in PurposeCam are stored inside the app by default. They are not automatically saved to your personal Photos library.

A photo is sent to Photos or another app only when you choose an action such as Save to Photos or Share. Photos and app data stored inside PurposeCam are removed from the device when you delete the app.

3. Ads

PurposeCam may use Google AdMob and Google User Messaging Platform to support the free app. These services may process device information, ad interactions, and consent status for ad delivery, measurement, frequency capping, and consent management according to Google's policies.

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Google AdMob Privacy

PurposeCam does not request App Tracking Transparency permission in the first release and does not access IDFA unless a future release adds tracking features with the required permission flow.

4. Permissions

You can change PurposeCam's Camera and Photos permissions at any time in iOS Settings. If a permission is denied, only features that require that permission may be limited.

5. Children

PurposeCam is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. Photos remain on the user's device unless the user chooses to share or save them outside the app.

6. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy if app features, advertising SDK behavior, or legal requirements change. Updates will be posted on this page.
